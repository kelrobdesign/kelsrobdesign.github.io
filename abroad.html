International Journalism




The Task


I took part in an immersive study abroad program where I learned the art of multimedia storytelling (writing, photography, + video) in Urbino, Italy. The program occurs in cooperation with The Institute for Education in International Media’s (ieiMedia) Urbino Program. As part of this program, I received basic language instruction and experience working alongside interpreters for fieldwork.




Major Takeaways


  • how to write for the web
  • how to take great digital photos
  • how to shoot and edit quality video
  • the ins and outs of intercultural communication
  • how to work as part of a production team



The Story


I was assigned to report on La Maestra Natura, an experimental, completely outdoor preschool nestled in the hills of the Marche region of Italy. I spent four weeks assisting at the school and gaining first-hand insight into the day to day operations.

I conducted weeks worth of in-depth interviews with constituents of the school and worked in collaboration with translators from a local university to ensure I would produce an accurate article. My work resulted in the creation of a detailed article which depicts the school’s community in a visionary way that encapsulates readers.




The Photos


I was mentored by Bob Reeder, a highly accredited photojournalist who has worked for The Washington Post and Politico + has been recognized by White House Press Photographers Association, National Press Photographers Association, as well as other organizations. Although studying abroad was my first time professionally behind a lens, through Reeder’s mentorship, I learned quickly and consistently produced impressive photographs.

I was tasked with taking headshot photos of my fellow colleagues, conducting branding photo sessions, as well as creating a photo story to accompany my written piece featuring La Maestra. My photo story was nominated for the best photo story award for the program.



The Video



I also got to take a peek behind the curtain into the world of videography. Through the program I gained a familiarity with audio fundamentals and film editing software. Each rookie videographer in the program (myself included) was paired up with a fellow amature colleague to produce a short video to accompany their individual story packages. As a result, I produced my first ever short video with a timely completion + delivery while learning to work with a production team.
